About Me

I didn't write my first line of code until my freshman year of college.

After graduating from Francis Scott Key High School in 2020, I took engineering classes at Virginia Tech and the University of Maryland. In these introduction to engineering classes I was introduced to my first programming language: MATLAB. I was amazed by how well it could process and manipulate the computational fluid dynamics (CFD) data from my groups's wind turbine project, and decided to explore more over the summer. Now, I am currently studying computer science at Towson University, where I am on track to graduate in 2025.

My interests include software design and development, data analysis, and data science. Lately I have been exploring creating mobile apps using Kotlin.

In my free time I enjoy going to the boxing gym, playing recreational soccer, and reading.
